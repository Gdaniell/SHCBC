Our History



1900s - 1930s

The women of St Hilda's College, were the first on the river, and started rowing in the early 1900's. The club purchased it's first VIII+ in 1911, which is now over 100 years ago.





1940s - 1968

Juliet Margaret Rogerson shares her experiences of rowing at St Hilda's and for O.U.W.B.C in 1946-7 along with some photographs from the time.





1969

St Hilda's Make Rowing History by becoming the first crew of women to ever compete in Oxford's Summer Eights.





1970s

There is very little information about St Hilda's Boat Club during this time, the Crew lists are unfortunately the only information available to us





1980s

In the 1980s, the world saw what St Hilda's rowers had to offer with 2 ex-St Hilda's rowers, Alison Gill and Pauline Janson becoming Olympians





1990s

St Hilda's Boat Club enjoyed a great deal of success in the 90s, including 1st Division blades and more than a scattering of St Hilda's representatives in OUWBC, OUBC and OUWLRC.





2000s

News is relatively sparse during this time, however 2008 seems to be a bumper year. In 2008 not only did we reach our highest position ever in Summer eights, 3rd place in Division 1, but our "Hildabeast Beer Boat" also won blades, and for the first time, St Hilda's entered a Men's crew in Summer Eights!





2010-2012

Some exciting wins for St Hilda's in external Regattas, for more recent updates please see our News section or our Gallery
